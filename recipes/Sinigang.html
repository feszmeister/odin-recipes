Pork Sinigang

What is Sinigang?

Sinigang is a tamarind-based soup that is known for its sour and savory taste. You can make it with either fish, pork belly, spare ribs or corned beef. This version of the recipe and the most common utilizes pork belly. The quality of this dish depends on the souring agent. This is the ingredient that makes the soup sour. The most common and widely used is unripe tamarind. It is known as sampaloc in the Philippines. Other fruits such as guava, tomato, bilimbi or kamias, green mango, pineapple, and wild mangosteen or santol can be used to make the sinigang taste sour.
