Sinangag Filipino Garlic Fried Rice

What is Sinangag?

Sinangag, or garlic fried rice, is the ultimate Filipino breakfast side dish. Made with day-old rice fried in oil and garlic, it's the best aroma to wake up to in the morning. The basic formula for fried rice is day-old white rice plus oil plus garlic. But more often than not, Filipinos add more ingredients to flesh out the dish and make it even more flavorful. Don't be surprised to find peas, carrots, corn, and bits of ham in your sinangag. Sinangag is most often served with a fried egg and a traditional meat dish such as tocino, tapa, or longganisa.

Ingredients

  • 3 Tbsp of desired oil
  • 12 cloves of garlic
  • 4 Cups of cooked rice, cooled and dried
  • 1/4 Tsp of salt
  • a dash of freshly ground pepper
  • 1 Scallion, thinly sliced,used for garnish
  • 4 Eggs, optional for serving!

Steps

  1. Using a large wok or skillet heat oil over medium/high heat
  2. Reduce heat to medium and add garlic, stirring till you have a light golden colour
  3. Carefully remove the garlic, leaving the garlic infused oil in the pan
  4. Now add the rice to the garlic infused oil, making sure to stir and coat the rice completely with the oil.
  5. Once coated, spread out for maximum surface area and let it cook for 3-5m undisturbed.
  6. Repeat the process of spreading out the rice and letting it cook for a couple minutes until the rice is cooked to your preference
  7. Once your rice is cooked as per your preference, dont overcook it or it will be very tough, return the majority of the removed garlic to the pan.
  8. Season rice with salt and pepper as desired, adding the scallion and any leftover garlic for garnish
  9. Add the egg if desired, or serve hot with your desired dish and enjoy!
